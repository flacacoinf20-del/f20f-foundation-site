Institutional Identity

A Global Hub for Transformation

F20F Foundation is an international institution dedicated to the structural transformation of the human being. Its operational architecture bridges the legal solidity of the United States with the resilient soul of Bolivia, creating an ecosystem where experience and potential converge to restore dignity.

The foundation is conceived as a serious institutional platform for transformation, discipline, leadership, and strategic humanitarian action.

Operational Structure

  • United States operational consolidation
  • Strategic focus in Maryland and Washington, D.C.
  • Institutional rigor and compliance orientation
  • International projection with social roots in Bolivia

Mission

The Transformational Mandate

Our mission is to transform lives through the structural reengineering of the human will using the proprietary Factor 20 methodology. We unite the potential of youth with the discipline of experienced leadership to restore dignity to animals, children, and the elderly.

We are committed to forming principled leaders, free from addictions, empowering women as agents of change, and defending media truth as a pillar of democratic integrity.

Factor 20 Method

Manifesto of Intrinsic Transformation

Factor 20 is based on the conviction that real transformation does not emerge from superficial repetition. It is a structural mutation of the will that takes place in the 20 silent days before visible change.

During this cycle, the individual moves from vulnerability to conscious self-power, preparing biologically, ethically, and spiritually for permanent transformation.

The 20-Day Architecture

Days 1–5 Foundation
Days 6–10 Ignition
Days 11–15 Social Structure
Days 16–20 Consolidation
Day 21 The Permanent Mutation

The Flaca Effect

The Mystique of Origin

Bolivia is the emotional heart of the foundation. The symbolic origin of the mission is the rescue of Flaca, a dog found in catastrophic condition and restored through love, care, and disciplined action.

That event became proof of principle: if life could be reconstructed in Flaca, dignity and leadership can be reconstructed in human beings.

“Love changes everything. It transforms everything.”
